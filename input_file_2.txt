Updates on India's 2024 Election Results: Narendra Modi's Third Term as Prime Minister
Narendra Modi is set to take the oath as Prime Minister of India for the third time today, following the General Elections of 2024. The swearing-in ceremony for Prime Minister Modi and the Council of Ministers is scheduled for June 9, 2024. Distinguished guests from India's neighboring countries and the Indian Ocean region have been invited to the ceremony.

Attending Dignitaries
Sri Lanka: President H.E. Mr. Ranil Wickremesinghe
Maldives: President H.E. Dr. Mohamed Muizzu
Seychelles: Vice-President H.E. Mr. Ahmed Afif
Bangladesh: Prime Minister H.E. Sheikh Hasina
Mauritius: Prime Minister H.E. Mr. Pravind Kumar Jugnauth
Nepal: Prime Minister H.E. Mr. Pushpa Kamal Dahal ‘Prachanda’
Bhutan: Prime Minister H.E. Mr. Tshering Tobgay
These leaders will also attend a banquet hosted by President Smt. Droupadi Murmu at Rashtrapati Bhavan the same evening. This visit underscores India's 'Neighbourhood First' policy and 'SAGAR' vision.

Modi's Political Journey
Shri Narendra Modi was first sworn in as Prime Minister on May 30, 2019, marking his second term. Born after India’s Independence, he has served as Prime Minister from 2014 to 2019 and was the longest-serving Chief Minister of Gujarat (October 2001 to May 2014).

Election Victories
In the 2014 and 2019 Parliamentary elections, Modi led the Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) to record wins with absolute majorities. In 2024, although the BJP did not secure a majority on its own, the BJP-led National Democratic Alliance (NDA) won 293 out of 543 seats, ensuring Modi's third term as Prime Minister.

Governance and Initiatives
Modi's tenure is marked by several transformative initiatives:

Sabka Saath, Sabka Vikas, Sabka Vishwas: Aimed at inclusive, corruption-free governance.
Antyodaya: Ensuring last-mile delivery of schemes and services.
Ayushman Bharat: World's largest healthcare program, covering over 50 crore Indians.
Pradhan Mantri Jan Dhan Yojana: Opened over 35 crore bank accounts for financial inclusion.
Pradhan Mantri Shram Yogi Man Dhan Yojana: Pension coverage for over 42 crore people in the unorganized sector.
Pradhan Mantri Ujjwala Yojana: Provided free cooking gas connections to over 7 crore beneficiaries.
Housing for All: Over 1.25 crore houses built between 2014 and 2019.
PM Kisan Samman Nidhi: Monetary incentives for farmers, expanded to all farmers in Modi's second term.
Swachh Bharat Mission: Raised sanitation coverage from 38% in 2014 to 99%.
Infrastructure and Development
Transportation: Next-generation infrastructure including highways, railways, and waterways.
Make in India: Boosted manufacturing, significantly increasing the number of mobile manufacturing units.
Ease of Doing Business: Improved India’s ranking from 142 in 2014 to 77 in 2019.
Goods and Services Tax (GST): Implemented 'One Nation, One Tax'.
Environmental Initiatives
Climate Change: Advocated for climate justice and launched the International Solar Alliance.
Swachh Bharat Mission: Recognized by the World Health Organization for potentially saving three lakh lives.
International Relations
Foreign Policy: Enhanced India's global role, attending major international summits and improving bilateral relations.
Honours: Received top awards from various countries including Saudi Arabia, Russia, and UAE.
Personal Background
Narendra Modi was born on September 17, 1950, in Vadnagar, Gujarat. He grew up in a modest family and joined the Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh (RSS) early in his life. Modi holds an M.A. in political science from Gujarat University. Known for his personal connect with people, Modi is also an avid writer and social media enthusiast.

Modi’s administration has been marked by a series of reforms and initiatives aimed at improving governance, infrastructure, and international relations, solidifying his reputation as a leader dedicated to India's development and prosperity.
